# Chapter 1, Zone 2B: The Industrial Wing (Architectural Blueprint)

**Zone Purpose:** A highly lethal, linear gauntlet that strips away the player's ability to run in circles. It forces reliance on precise Bio-Kinetic platforming, narrow choke points, and vertical escapes. 

---

## Room 1: The Airlock (The Pressure Chamber)
*   **Tactical Purpose:** A claustrophobic staging area that introduces the sheer stopping power needed for this wing.
*   **Atmosphere:** Heavy, scarred steel bulkheads. Failing decontamination sprayers leak thick, freezing coolant mist into the air. Flashing red warning strobes provide the only illumination.
*   **Interactables:** The Rivet-Repeater wallbuy is mounted right by the entrance. The *Ion-Burst Pistol* (Tier 6) is available deeper in this wing (see `Weapons_and_Economy.md` / `Map_Chapter-1`). 
*   **The Vampire Advantage (Decompression Blowback):** The room is still semi-pressurized. Players can shoot the manual override panels on the doors to trigger a massive, localized decompression wave.
*   **The Escape Loop:** The decompression wave doesn't deal damage, but it violently knocks all Scrap-Walkers and Hunter-Killers to the floor for a few seconds. This gives the player the exact window needed to Wall-Kick over the downed machines and push into the next room.
*   **Machine Ingress:** Floor grates that snap open, releasing machines directly at the player's feet.

---

## Room 2: The Geothermal Trench (The Chasm)
*   **Tactical Purpose:** The primary death zone of the wing. A completely linear push with massive environmental hazards.
*   **Atmosphere:** Deafening and searing hot. A massive, bottomless trench cuts through the center of the room, glowing with raw geothermal energy. Players navigate via narrow, broken metal catwalks spanning the gap. 
*   **Interactables:** The Crimson Chamber perk (Double Tap) sits on an exposed, isolated platform in the center of the trench. A 3D Fabricator spawn point is tucked against the far wall. The Mag-Crushers trap is located at the main choke point.
*   **The Vampire Advantage (Catwalk Vaulting):** Machines are bound by rigid pathfinding and must walk the linear, winding catwalks. Vampires are not. 
*   **The Escape Loop:** If a Vanguard unit blocks the narrow path ahead, the player can Vault-Boost directly across the open chasm to a parallel catwalk. If completely cornered, a player can drop off the edge, slide down a diagonal support strut, and use their Kinetic Heat momentum to launch back up behind the enemy lines.
*   **Machine Ingress:** Magnetic Adhesion. Machines do not use doors here; they continuously crawl up from the glowing depths of the trench and pull themselves over the railings, flanking the player constantly.

---

## Room 3: The Boiler Room (The Vertical Holdout)
*   **Tactical Purpose:** A multi-level holdout zone requiring players to constantly shift elevation before breaking through to Zone 3 (The Mag-Train Platform).
*   **Atmosphere:** An oppressive, towering room filled with massive, vibrating cylindrical boilers. High-pressure steam obscures vision, and sparks rain down from severed catwalk cables above.
*   **Interactables:** The Kinetic DMR wallbuy is on the second-floor railing. A 3D Fabricator spawn point sits beneath the main stairwell. Gate D (the heavy exit to Zone 3 Mag-Train) is on the ground floor.
*   **The Vampire Advantage (Thermal Vents):** The ground floor is dotted with ruptured, high-pressure steam vents that can be manipulated by the players.
*   **The Escape Loop:** Machines cannot use the steam vents and must slowly trudge up the metal staircases to reach the second floor. A vampire can slide directly over a ruptured vent to be instantly blasted upward onto the second-story catwalks, completely breaking machine line-of-sight and giving them a perfect vantage point to rain fire down on the horde.
*   **Machine Ingress:** Heavy industrial cargo elevators. Instead of vents, the elevators periodically "ding" and open, releasing tightly clustered, heavily armored Vanguard squads directly onto the floor.
